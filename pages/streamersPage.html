Streamers

A streamer is defined as the person who broadcasts live from any of the platforms that offer this service. Among these, we can highlight Twitch, YouTube Live, Facebook Gaming, Booyah, Trovo and NimoTV. Streamers often collaborate with each other, sending their followers to another user's channel, in order to increase their audiences with similar interests to theirs. Cooperativity is one of its characteristics to highlight. Among the most famous streamers worldwide, we can mention the Spanish AuronPlay, who has 11.8 million followers on Twitch, followed by El Rubius (11.2 million) and Ibai Llanos (9.4). In Argentina, Coscu stands out with 3.4 million followers, Markito Navaja 1.8 and Momo Benavides with 862 thousand.

Twitch is an American platform belonging to the company Amazon, Inc., which allows live broadcasts. This platform's main function is to broadcast video games live, a field in which it has YouTube as a competitor. Billed as a by-product of another general interest streaming platform, the site primarily focuses on gaming, including user-played game playthroughs, eSports streaming, and other gaming-related events.

YouTube Live is a feature of YouTube that allows you to stream live video, just like Facebook Live, Periscope, and Livestream. These videos can be seen directly on the channel of the user who transmits them. They have a chat that also allows you to interact with people connected at that time.
